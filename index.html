In A Nutshell



I am a student in Vanderbilt's Coding Bootcamp. I have two daughters, Ophelia, 4, and Leona, 9 months. My wife Kelsie and I met in High School in West Springfield, Massachusetts, and together we have travelled far and wide, currently residing amongst the leafy confines of Donelson, a neighborhood in northeast Nashville, where we share a somewhat rambling brick house from the middle of last centruy with a dog named Elvis, 3 cats too nasty for naming and 1200 vinyl records. The focus of the collection is primarily on soul, funk, blues, and country. I work in the United Record Pressing warehouse on Allied Drive, not far from Phonoluxe Records, Nashville's best and longest-operating independent record store. My ultimate and inchoate goal is to find a way to transfer the immediacy and permanence of physical media with the realities of an increasingly digital world.
